thomasTrenholme/greatBarrierReef

    • Partnering with Google and their Tensorflow library, Kaggle.com has offered a competition to help Australia's Great Barrier Reef. This competition requires the use of a Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) and attempts to challenge entrants in creating the most accurate way to successfully identify and recognize Crown Of Thorns Starfish (COTS) from a dataset of underwater images. Because of the amount of noise and suboptimal processing conditions of the images, pre-processing and taking a targeted approach in training the CNN is extremely important. Google has pledged to take the winning code and use it in conjunction with its team to remove this invasive starfish species from Australia's Great Barrier Reef.

    My partner, Kellen Sabhlok, and I are in the process of creating and training an accurate CNN to fit the needs of this competition and allow us to identify the COTS within these underwater images. Currently, we are researching many different approaches to this problem and comparing and contrasting the available strategies that exist to deal with image processing situations similar to this.
